Trading in the real world.

In order to start making safe investments, or at least with a minified risk, you'll need a lot of tools under your belt, you should at least have a basic knowledge about Technical Analysis, Fundamental Analysis or a combination of both, and both themes are quite broad to get the grasps.

If you're a newbie, you could start taking a look at Investopedia resources for technical analysis, it will take you a while to get the grasps of the fundamentals but you'll be in the right path. You should then start analyzing historical graphical data and simulating "paper" investments.

Eventually, you'll be confident enough to start risking your money on real-world trades, and yet you're almost guaranteed to make several loses on your first trades.

Whatever you do, you should stay away from "advises" from those YouTubers who assure you should buy X coin which is going to the moon and you'll get rich in a year or so.

You see, generally, these guys have already bought those coins at a very low price, then they (and several other people) start talking about and promoting the coins, then a lot of people, usually newbies, start buying those coins rapidly which increases the coin price, this is when those "influencers" can sell their cut for a very nice profit. Large amounts of coins sold usually make the prices crash and then all of those who followed those advises will incur in loses.
